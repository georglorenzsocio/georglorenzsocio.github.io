## Overview

My research examines how social differences become socially consequential, how they structure social relationships, and what consequences this has for integration, social cohesion, and social inequality. Situated at the intersection of sociology, social stratification research, and the sociology of education, my work investigates these processes against the backdrop of major societal transformations, including increasing diversity, migration, and digitalization.

Across my work, I combine social-structural, network-theoretical, and analytical-sociological perspectives with advanced quantitative methods, longitudinal data, social network analysis, simulations, and computational approaches. Schools play a central role in this agenda because they function as microcosms of broader societal transformations, allowing fundamental sociological processes to be studied in a particularly visible form.

## 1. Social Differentiation and the Emergence of Social Boundaries
A first line of research examines when social differences become socially relevant. I investigate how social categories such as ethnicity, social origin, gender, language, and migration background shape perceptions, stereotypes, discrimination, and social boundaries. My work has shown how subtle forms of discrimination emerge through routine judgments and expectations, contributing to educational inequalities even in the absence of explicit prejudice. Building on classical theories of social structure and heterogeneity, I currently develop the concept of multidimensional heterogeneity to understand how multiple dimensions of social differentiation jointly influence social relationships, group boundaries, social cohesion, and inequality.

## 2. Social Relationships as Mechanisms of Integration and Inequality
A second line of research investigates how social relationships translate social differences into integration, social cohesion, and inequality. My work examines both the formation and consequences of friendships, social networks, and social capital. I have studied how diversity shapes social integration and interethnic friendships, how network structures influence access to resources and opportunities, and how peer processes contribute to the reproduction or reduction of inequality. Current and future work extends these questions beyond school contexts and examines how social relationships change across major life-course transitions such as entry into higher education, work, and family formation.

## 3. Digital Transformation of Social Relationships
A third line of research explores how digital technologies transform the formation, maintenance, and consequences of social relationships. I investigate how digital and offline networks intersect, whether digital interactions complement or replace traditional forms of social embeddedness, and how these developments affect integration, trust, social cohesion, and inequality. A particular focus lies on generative AI and large language models as novel interaction partners. Through innovative data-donation approaches and longitudinal survey data, I examine how digital technologies reshape social relationships and whether they contribute to new forms of inequality, social isolation, or social integration.
